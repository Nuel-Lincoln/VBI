Course Title

The Digestive System

Average Age

15 Years

Entry Behaviour

The teacher asks the students in the collaborative group what they ate before coming to school. H/she further asked the students to state what they think happened to the food they ate.

Test Of Entry Behaviour

Students in the collaborative group are asked to share their experiences that relates to what they ate before coming to school and how such food gives them energy to learn in class.

Instructional Material

Use of Virtual Biology Instruction (VBI), Explanation, use of examples, Illustration, questioning, reinforcement, activity and stimulus variation.

Instructional Techniques

Use of VBI, Questioning and Reinforcement.

General Course Objectives

At the end of this course, the students should be able to do the following
  1. Define the term Digestion
  2. Differentiate between digestion and respiration
  3. Discuss the Digestive system
  4. Discuss the process of digestion in the food ate today
  5. Discuss the alimentary tract of earthworm
  6. List the three (3) organs that helps in digestion in Planaria
  7. Differentiate between the alimentary canals of bird and grasshopper
  8. List the ten (10) alimentary tracts in man
  9. Outline the enzymes responsible for digestion in man
  10. Draw and label the digestive systems in man
  11. Differentiate between the digestive organs in bird and man
  12. Discuss the mechanism of feeding in tapeworm
  13. List 2 animals that uses the grinding mechanism for feeding
  14. Differentiate between sucking and absorbing mechanism of feeding
  15. Draw and label the human tooth

The Digestive System

A digestive system is a group of organs consisting of the central gastrointestinal (GI) tract and its associated accessory organs that break down food into smaller components so that nutrients can be absorbed and assimilated. This provides the necessary energy to sustain the body.
