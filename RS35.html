Course Title

The Respiratory System

Average Age

15 Years

Entry Behaviour

Students are in the previous lesson learnt about the mechanism of respiration in lower and higher animals and the various respiratory organs. It is expected that they have rudimentary ideas on the mechanism of respiration in man.

Test of Entry Behaviour

Students are asked to describe the mechanism of respiration in amoeba. Students are asked to share their knowledge on the respiration in man.

Instructional Material

Use of Virtual Biology Instruction (VBI), Explanation, use of examples, Illustration, questioning, reinforcement, activity and stimulus variation.

Instructional Techniques

Use of VBI, Questioning and Reinforcement.

General Course Objectives

At the end of this course, the students should be able to do the following
  1. define the term cellular respiration
  2. discuss the phases of respiration
  3. state the conditions necessary for respiration
  4. differentiate between inspiration and expiration
  5. list the different respiratory systems in animals
  6. state the characteristics of respiratory surface
  7. draw and label the respiratory systems in man
  8. differentiate between the respiratory organs in animal and plants
  9. Discuss the mechanism of respiration in man
  10. discuss the mechanism of respiration in Amoeba and Paramecium
  11. discuss respiration in insects
  12. differentiate between skin or coetaneous and lung respiration
  13. Discuss the meaning of respiratory organs
  14. Differentiate between the gills and lung book
  15. Explain the functions of the body surface as an organ of respiration
  16. Explain the process of inspiration and expiration in man
  17. Discuss the mechanism of respiration in plant
  18. Draw and label the respiratory system in plants
  19. Compare breathing mechanism in stomata and lenticels
  20. Explain the process of respiration flowering plants
